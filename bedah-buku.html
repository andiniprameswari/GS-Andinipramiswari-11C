Bedah Buku: 101 Essays that Will Change the Way You Think

Ditulis oleh: Brianna Wiest

101 Essays That Will Change the Way You Think by Brianna Wiest is a collection of short, reflective essays that challenge limiting beliefs and explore self-awareness, emotions, healing, and personal growth, encouraging readers to rethink how they see themselves and the world.

"You have to stop believing that you need other people's permission to be okay with yourself."

That quote means your self-worth shouldn’t depend on approval from others. You don’t need validation, agreement, or permission to accept yourself, make choices, or feel at peace with who you are. Waiting for others to “approve” keeps your confidence in their hands. The moment you decide you’re okay with yourself, you take that power back. In short: self-acceptance is an inside job.

What makes the book stand out is its gentle but confronting tone. Many essays challenge common mindsets such as chasing external validation, fearing discomfort, or resisting change. The writing is philosophical yet accessible, making it easy to read slowly, revisit, and reflect on over time.

Overall, the book is less about “changing your life overnight” and more about changing the way you think, so healthier choices and perspectives naturally follow. It’s especially appealing to readers who enjoy introspective, emotional, and mindset-focused self-help rather than practical productivity guides.

Bedah Buku: The Laws of Power

Ditulis oleh: Robert Greene

The 48 Laws of Power by Robert Greene explores the hidden rules that shape influence, control, and authority in human relationships. The book argues that power is not something officially given, but something constantly negotiated through behavior, perception, and strategy.

Greene shows that people rarely act purely out of kindness or fairness; instead, ego, fear, ambition, and self-interest often drive decisions. By studying historical figures who rose or fell because of how they handled power, the book reveals that success often depends on knowing when to speak, when to stay silent, when to stand out, and when to blend in.

"Never outshine the master."

It means you shouldn’t make someone more powerful than you feel inferior or threatened by your abilities. Outshining them can trigger jealousy and backlash, so it’s often smarter to show respect and restraint until the right moment.

Rather than encouraging cruelty, the book emphasizes awareness. It suggests that many people are harmed not because they are weak, but because they are unaware of the unspoken games around them.

Understanding power helps a person protect their reputation, manage emotions, and avoid being manipulated or underestimated. At the same time, Greene acknowledges that power is dangerous if used recklessly, as every action creates reactions and enemies. The book ultimately presents power as a tool: it can be used defensively to survive complex social environments or offensively to gain control, but it always requires discipline, observation, and restraint.
